Korean life is incomplete without soups! In America, most soups have some sort of a flavorful base that thickens the soup. In Korea, the base is water and the flavoring is spice. Boiled kimchi (kimchi jjigae) is a popular soup in South Korea. Deonjeong Jjigae is a fermented soybean soup that smells awful but tastes delicious. Tteokguk is a traditional soup that Koreans eat on New Years. Fun fact: In the Korean language they say people "eat" their age. Tteokguk is the dish that makes you a year older!
